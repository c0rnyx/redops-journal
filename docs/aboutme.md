# About Me

---

Hi, I'm a cybersecurity enthusiast with a primary focus on offensive security, penetration testing, and security assessment. Through hands-on laboratories, Capture The Flag challenges, and independent research, I continuously develop practical skills in identifying, exploiting, and documenting security vulnerabilities across Linux, Windows, web applications, and Active Directory environments.

While offensive security is my primary area of interest, I view penetration testing as a means of improving defensive security rather than simply compromising systems. My long-term objective is to work in a Purple Team environment where offensive techniques directly contribute to stronger detections, improved monitoring, and more resilient security architectures.

This portfolio documents that journey through professional client-style penetration test reports, technical walkthroughs, and research notes.

---

# My Approach

I believe technical ability is only one part of penetration testing.

A successful penetration test should clearly demonstrate:

- how a vulnerability was identified,
- why it exists,
- its potential business impact,
- and how it should be remediated.

For that reason, every penetration test report within this portfolio follows a standardized methodology inspired by professional client deliverables rather than traditional CTF writeups.

Equally important is understanding the defensive perspective. Every finding includes remediation guidance, encouraging a mindset that extends beyond exploitation toward improving detection capabilities, hardening systems, and reducing organizational risk. My goal is to bridge offensive and defensive security by producing reports that are technically accurate, actionable, and valuable to both security teams and stakeholders.

# Security Philosophy

**Offensive security is most valuable when it improves defensive security.**

Every assessment included in this portfolio focuses not only on identifying vulnerabilities, but also on understanding why they exist, evaluating their potential impact, and providing practical remediation guidance.

I believe effective cybersecurity professionals should understand both how systems are compromised and how they can be monitored, hardened, and defended. This portfolio reflects that philosophy by combining realistic attack simulation with standardized reporting, defensive recommendations, and a strong emphasis on improving an organization's overall security posture.
